📋 After-Disaster Tips

What to do after floods, fires, earthquakes

After a Flood
  • Wait for official all-clear before returning home
  • Watch for downed power lines
  • Don't walk through flowing water (just 6 inches can knock you down)
  • Check for structural damage before entering
  • Document damage with photos for insurance
  • Throw away food that came in contact with floodwater
  • Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
  • Watch for mold growth - dry within 24-48 hours
After a Fire
  • Don't re-enter until authorities say it's safe
  • Be careful of structural damage
  • Watch for hot spots that could re-ignite
  • Don't use electrical appliances until checked
  • Save undamaged possessions for cleaning
  • Contact your insurance company immediately
  • Beware of respiratory issues from smoke damage
  • Secure your property from weather and theft
After an Earthquake
  • Expect aftershocks - they can cause additional damage
  • Check yourself and others for injuries
  • Put on sturdy shoes to avoid broken glass
  • Check gas, water, electrical lines for damage
  • If you smell gas, turn off main valve and leave
  • Listen to radio for emergency information
  • Stay away from damaged buildings
  • Use text messages, not calls, to conserve network
After a Cyclone
  • Stay indoors until official all-clear
  • Watch for fallen power lines and trees
  • Be careful of flooded roads while driving
  • Check on neighbors, especially elderly
  • Boil water until declared safe
  • Document property damage for insurance
  • Be cautious of wild animals displaced by storm
  • Help with community cleanup if safe
During a Heatwave
  • Stay in air-conditioned buildings
  • Drink plenty of water (don't wait until thirsty)
  • Wear lightweight, light-colored clothing
  • Never leave children or pets in cars
  • Check on elderly family and neighbors
  • Avoid strenuous outdoor activity
  • Watch for signs of heat exhaustion
  • Use fans and take cool showers

Flood Safety

Turn Around, Don't Drown. Just 6 inches of water can knock you down.

Fire Safety

Stop, Drop, and Roll. Have two escape routes from every room.

Earthquake Safety

Drop, Cover, and Hold On. Stay away from windows.
